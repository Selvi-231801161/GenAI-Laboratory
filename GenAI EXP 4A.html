Historical Eras

Five Decades of
Price Evolution

Each era reshaped the global energy economy with distinct political, technological, and market forces.

1970–1979
The Shock Decade
The 1973 OPEC oil embargo triggered the first major global oil crisis, sending gasoline prices up 350% overnight. The Iranian Revolution in 1979 sparked a second shock. Western nations scrambled for energy independence.
Crude: $3 → $35/bbl
1980–1989
Crash & Adjustment
A global recession dampened demand. OPEC lost market control as non-member production surged. The 1986 price collapse saw crude fall below $10/bbl. Nations diversified energy sources and improved fuel efficiency standards.
Crude: $35 → $15/bbl
1990–1999
Gulf War Spike
The Gulf War caused a short-lived price spike in 1990. Subsequent years remained stable with low prices, supporting strong economic growth. The decade ended with an Asian financial crisis depressing demand.
Crude: $15 → $25/bbl
2000–2009
The Super Cycle
China's industrial boom drove unprecedented demand. Crude hit an all-time high of $147/bbl in July 2008. The global financial crisis then triggered the steepest price collapse in history — a 70% drop in six months.
Crude: $25 → $147 → $32/bbl
2010–2019
Shale Revolution
US shale oil production transformed global supply dynamics. Prices recovered to $100+ before OPEC's 2014 decision to defend market share caused a dramatic crash. A new era of volatile, lower prices took hold.
Crude: $80 → $115 → $30/bbl
2020–2026
Pandemic & Transition
COVID-19 briefly pushed crude prices negative in April 2020. A rapid recovery led to 2022's energy crisis driven by Russia-Ukraine conflict. Green energy transition pressure and EV adoption began permanently reshaping fuel demand.
Crude: −$37 → $130 → $82/bbl

Major Events

Oil Price Shocks
& Market Crises

Events that caused sudden, dramatic movements in global fuel prices, reshaping economies and energy policies.

1973
OPEC Oil Embargo
Arab OPEC members cut supply to nations supporting Israel. Overnight, prices quadrupled and petrol rationing swept the US and Europe.
+350%
1979
Iranian Revolution
The Islamic Revolution halted Iranian exports, reducing global supply by 4%. Combined with Iraq–Iran War, prices surged to record levels for the era.
+150%
1986
OPEC Market Share War
Saudi Arabia flooded markets to recapture share from non-OPEC producers. Crude collapsed from $31 to under $10 within months.
–68%
2008
Super Spike + Financial Crash
Crude hit $147.27 in July driven by speculation and demand. The global financial crisis then crashed prices 70% by December — the fastest collapse in history.
–70%
2020
COVID-19 Pandemic
Global lockdowns eliminated demand. WTI futures turned negative (–$37.63/bbl) as storage hit capacity. The most extreme price event in oil market history.
–300%
2022
Russia–Ukraine War
Sanctions on Russia's energy exports triggered Europe's worst energy crisis in decades. Brent surged to $130/bbl; gas prices hit generational highs globally.
+78%
